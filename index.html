  • <h3>Facebook</h3> At <a href="http://www.facebook.com" target="_blank">Facebook</a>, I worked as a hardware engineering intern on the server team's first energy-efficient server design in 2010. My day often consists of my manager dumping 10 servers for me in the morning and asking me to diagnose what went wrong in each of them (and fix them if I can). This project eventually became the <a href="http://opencompute.org/" target="_blank">Open Compute Project</a>, with Facebook releasing the <a href="http://opencompute.org/specs/" target="_blank">specs and designs</a> of the server and datacenters. Specifically, I worked on testing memory, CPU energy usage, and various hardware components on the motherboard. The new system reduced the power consumption by 38% and costs 24% less than the previous models.

    Facebook

  • <h3>Hewlett-Packard</h3> I joined the Workstation Global Business Unit's Taiwan Design Center (TDC) at <a href="http://www.hp.com" target="_blank">Hewlett-Packard</a> as a Research & Development intern in 2009.
