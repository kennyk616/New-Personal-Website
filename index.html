I was born in Taipei, Taiwan, lived in Tianjin, China for 5 and 1/2 years, and attended George School, in Newtown, PA, from 10th - 12th grades before coming to California for college. While at Stanford as an undergrad, I was a Project Director for Stanford Consulting (SC), Vice Chair for Green Alliance for Innovative Action (GAIA), held various board positions for Asia-Pacific Student Entrepreneurship Society (ASES), and was a member of the Phi Kappa Psi Fraternity California Beta chapter. I am fluent in Mandarin and English, as well as conversational in Taiwanese (Hokkian). I enjoy working out, traveling, and am a huge LA Lakers fan.


Fun fact: I have an asteroid (mini-planet) named after me, called Chunkaikao - technically the full name is 24133 Chunkaikao (1999 VW67) - from being selected as one of the finalists for the Intel Science Talent Search (STS) with my project on fuel cell efficiency once upon a time.
