Projects

  • Technical Projects
  • Business Consulting

  • Droids in Space

    My team worked with NASA to investigate and implement a visual matching algorithm for estimation of relative motion of the camera. This is an importent step to implementing visual robot navigation system for NASA robots in space stations. For more information, check out our poster (shown on the top) and source code on github!

  • Youmote

    Youmote is a Kinect-powered TV controller that does what you want before you ask for it! My team also won #2 at the CS247: Human-Computer Interaction Studio final competition. Check out our project website, presentation video, and source code!

  • Kinect Gesture Recognizer

    My team implemented two selection gestures with the Microsoft Kinect. Our application area of interest was the party dance floor, and we created two gestures for both the dancers and the DJ. Our project was voted #1 in class at CS247: Human-Computer Interaction Studio! Check out our project website, presentation video, and source code!

  • Nom

    Nom is a mobile web application prototype that promotes healthy eating on Stanford campus. Unfortunately the university dining services does not have a good web api to automate the menu imports, so the website currently has no menu on it. For more information, please check out our website, source code, and poster.

  • Music Genre Classification

    My team investigated various machine learning algorithms, including k-nearest neighbor (k-NN), k-means, multi-class SVM, and neural networks to classify the following four genres: classical, jazz, metal, and pop. We implemented the algorithms in Matlab, and achieved a 90%+ success rate with some of the algorithms. For more information, check out our paper, poster, and source code!

Hi! My name is Chun-Kai Kao, but it's probably easier to just call me Ken. I graduated from Stanford University with a B.S. in Electrical Engineering in 2012. I am currently pursuing an M.S. in Electrical Engineering, focusing on Human-Computer Interaction and Artificial Intelligence. I will likely take a leave of absence in the 2012-2013 school year to join the Taiwanese army, and return for the 2013-2014 school year to finish my masters degree. In the future I hope to combine my interests in cleantech and software to help make the world a better place. Check out my previous technical and consulting projects in the projects section!

I am currently working as a Forward Deployed Engineering Intern at Palantir Technologies. Most of you may not be familiar with this position, so if you want to learn more, check out What is the day-to-day of a Forward Deployed Engineer at Palantir? and What does a Business Development Engineer at Palantir do and how is it different from a software engineer?.


Previously, I have interned at Opower (Arlington VA) as a Product Management and Software Engineering intern in 2011, Facebook (Palo Alto, CA) as a Hardware Engineering intern in 2010, and Hewlett-Packard (Taipei, Taiwan) as a Research & Development Engineering intern in 2009.


Check out a copy of my resume here.

I was born in Taipei, Taiwan, lived in Tianjin, China for 5 and 1/2 years, and attended George School, in Newtown, PA, from 10th - 12th grades before coming to California for college. While at Stanford as an undergrad, I was a Project Director for Stanford Consulting (SC), Vice Chair for Green Alliance for Innovative Action (GAIA), held various board positions for Asia-Pacific Student Entrepreneurship Society (ASES), and was a member of the Phi Kappa Psi Fraternity California Beta chapter. I am fluent in Mandarin and English, as well as conversational in Taiwanese (Hokkian). I enjoy working out, traveling, and am a huge LA Lakers fan.


Fun fact: I have an asteroid (mini-planet) named after me, called Chunkaikao - technically the full name is 24133 Chunkaikao (1999 VW67) - from being selected as one of the finalists for the Intel Science Talent Search (STS) with my project on fuel cell efficiency once upon a time.
