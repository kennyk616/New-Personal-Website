Projects

  • Droids in Space

    My team worked with NASA to investigate and implement a visual matching algorithm for estimation of relative motion of the camera. This is an importent step to implementing visual robot navigation system for NASA robots in space stations. For more information, check out our poster (shown on the top) and source code on github!

  • Youmote

    Youmote is a Kinect-powered TV controller that does what you want before you ask for it! My team also won #2 at the CS247: Human-Computer Interaction Studio final competition. Check out our project website, presentation video, and source code!

  • Kinect Gesture Recognizer

    My team implemented two selection gestures with the Microsoft Kinect. Our application area of interest was the party dance floor, and we created two gestures for both the dancers and the DJ. Our project was voted #1 in class at CS247: Human-Computer Interaction Studio! Check out our project website, presentation video, and source code!

  • Nom

    Nom is a mobile web application prototype that promotes healthy eating on Stanford campus. Unfortunately the university dining services does not have a good web api to automate the menu imports, so the website currently has no menu on it. For more information, please check out our website, source code, and poster.

  • Music Genre Classification

    My team investigated various machine learning algorithms, including k-nearest neighbor (k-NN), k-means, multi-class SVM, and neural networks to classify the following four genres: classical, jazz, metal, and pop. We implemented the algorithms in Matlab, and achieved a 90%+ success rate with some of the algorithms. For more information, check out our paper, poster, and source code!

  • Grupo RBS

    Grupo RBS, the 2nd largest media group in Brazil, is looking to establish the entrepreneurial environment in Brazil. I led a team of 4 graduate and undergraduate student consultants from Stanford Consulting to help RBS establish a venture capitalist branch. We spent a week in Brazil doing field work, as well as three months at Stanford and the Silicon Valley. We presented our recommendation to the executive team at Grupo RBS Digital Holdings, and our partnership has been featured in various Brazilian newspapers.

  • Google

    I led a team of 4 other graduate and undergraduate student consultants from Stanford Consulting to work with 2 Google advisors on identifying the most promising future revenue models in the mobile applications space. We analyzed the mobile applications through research articles, gathering raw data from downloading the top applications on the iTunes Store and Android Market, as well as interview over 20 professional and student developers and businessmen in the mobile application space.

  • EnerNOC

    EnerNOC is a leading demand response and energy management company. Previously, EnerNOC had full-time analysts manually looking through energy usage charts and marking the anomalies. My team from Stanford Consulting wrote a software in Matlab and Java to identify the anomolies in energy usage in a fast and consistent manner. We helped save the analysts' times by taking away the quantitative part of the analysis and allowing them to focus on the analytical aspects.

  • Silicon Valley Community Foundation

    Silicon Valley Community Foundation (SVCF) is a $1.7 Billion dollar foundation that helps individuals and organizations donate efficiently while focusing on their charitable passions. My team from Stanford Consulting came up with a market-entry strategy for SVCF to partner with small and medium-sized businesses. We concluded our project by presenting our findings and recommendation to board-room-level executives.

  • Coupa Cafe

    I worked with a team of 5 in Stanford Marketing to help market Coupa Cafe's opening at Stanford University's Campus between Green and Meyer libraries. We helped devise strategies to increase the popularity of Coupa Cafe on campus, as well as organized the Grand Opening on April 6, which included a live band, free coffees, and surprise secret coupons.

  • The Zen Mobile

    My team took a class called Creativity and Innovation, offered by the Design School (d.school) at Stanford. Our final project was to redesign the driving experience, so we came up with The Zen Mobile --a car that gravitates your emotions towards Zen. Check out our video at the top or on youtube.
